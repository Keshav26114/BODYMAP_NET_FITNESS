How it saves — one row per week

Every test you submit is stored against a calendar week (Monday–Sunday), not a single moment in time. If you submit more than once in the same week, the latest submission for that week is what counts — it doesn't create a duplicate row.

That week-based structure is what the streaks above are built from: they walk backwards from your most recent logged week and count how many consecutive calendar weeks in a row a metric stayed good (or bad). A week you skip entirely — or simply didn't retake a given test in — breaks the streak, the same as a bad week would.

Every row below is a permanent record: the exact numbers you entered, the model's prediction at the time, and (if you ran it) that week's Behaviour Profile. Click View on any row to see the full report again, including the tips and goal-verdict banner as they were computed for that specific week.
