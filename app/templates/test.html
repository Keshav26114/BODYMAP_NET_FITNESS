Model components

Five small neural networks work together, and every one of them now also takes your stated goal as an input, not just your measurements:

  • Exercise — weekly sets per muscle group → Under-training / Balanced / Over-training.
  • BMI — height, weight, age, gender → Underweight / Normal / Overweight / Obese.
  • Body Fat — neck, waist (and hip) measurements → a five-band body-fat read from Essential to Obese.
  • Ideal Calories — activity level and current intake → Under-eating / On-target / Over-eating relative to your Mifflin-St Jeor target.
  • Behaviour Profile (fusion) — combines whichever of the four embeddings you produced, using a learned placeholder for any test you skipped rather than a zero, so it degrades gracefully with 1, 2, 3, or 4 tests present. Your goal is concatenated in right before the final decision, so the same body composition can genuinely be classified differently depending on what you're training for.

The individual bands (BMI, body fat, calories, exercise) still reflect objective physical thresholds — Overweight is Overweight regardless of your goal. It's the Behaviour Profile, and the tips/streaks built on top of these results, that judge each result relative to your goal.
